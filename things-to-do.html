The Parlament and the Danube from Castle-hill

Things to do

A cruise on the Danube

High above the river Danube, on the top of Castle Hill sits this beatuful palace complex, once the home of Hungarian Kings. Originally built in 1265 you can see out across Budapest in all directions from the Castle walls.

Inside the Castle you can visit function rooms and the office of the Archduke. You can also see the bedrooms of the Archduke, the Archduchess, and their children. The Buda old town is next to the castle. The old town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. You can also visit the Matthias Church on top of Buda Castle hill.

Visit one of the famous thermal baths

The Fisherman's Bastion was built in the 19th century. There are 7 turrets which represent the 7 founding tribes of Hungary. The Guild of the Fishermen use to protect the area which is where its' name originates from. Walk along the walls and take in the view across the river Danube to the Parliament and Pest side of the city.

Many people stop here for a coffee on the terrace and a selfie. The architecture and view come together to create the perfect backdrop for a special occasion.

Wine Tasting

Budapest Zoo is situated in the Botanical gardens. It's one of the oldest zoos in Europe. Many of the buildings have been restored with their original Romantic architecture.

Now you can enjoy a perfect fun packed family day out! See tropical worlds, beautiful giant butterflies and a sea life demonstration. Climb the stairs to the top of Castle Rock and you'll get another unique view of the cityscape.

The zoo has recently developed it's children's play area complete with ride on miniature railway!
